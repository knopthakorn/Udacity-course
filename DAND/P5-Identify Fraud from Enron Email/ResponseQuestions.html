We will use machine learning to build the prediction model for identifying the likeness of someone with a particular pattern of being a POI or non-POI.

The dataset in this project there are 146 individuals which only 18 are persons of interest and contain 20 features and 1 taget.

5. What is validation, and what’s a classic mistake you can make if you do it wrong? How did you validate your analysis? [relevant rubric item: “validation strategy”]¶

Validation is the process to evaluate the algorithm performance with datset splitting into training and testing sets. We'll use the training set to train our algorithm, after that we'll evaluate the performance of our model with the testing set, that has not seen previously. By this process we can validated the performance of our algorithm to generalize its conclusions for the new data. If we do not validat our algorithm previously then our model may be very likely to overfiting on the new dataset that it has not observations previously.

With small dataset (18 POI and 125 non-POI after removing outliers), I used StratifiedShuffleSplit to split 50% of data into training set and put remaing 50% into testing sets to avoid overfitting and robust result.

Then to validation final algorithm, I used test_classifier function in tester.py for testing the algorithm to ensure that each data point got the chance in testing process, so that validation results along dataset dont depend on any specific pattern or characteristics of the dataset.

6. Give at least 2 evaluation metrics and your average performance for each of them. Explain an interpretation of your metrics that says something human-understandable about your algorithm’s performance. [relevant rubric item: “usage of evaluation metrics”]¶

I have used 4 evaluation metrics accuracy, precision, recall, and the F1-score. These metrics are based on predictive comparing of POI/non-POI to the actual.

Accuracy is the number of correct answers the model produced.

Precision is the number of times positively identifies a data point (True positive) divided by the total number of positive identifications (regardless of whether they were correct). High number of precision reflact POIs identified by the algorithm tended to be correct, while a low number means there were more failure to identifies non-POIs as POIs.

Recall is the number of true positives divided by the sum of the true positives and the number of incorrectly negatively identified a data point (false negative). Higher recall reflacted the algorithm do a good job of identifying the POIs in the test set, while lower recal reflact ther incorrectly labeling a POI as a non-POI.

F1 score is average weighted of precision and recall, calculated by multiplying the product of the recall and precision by two, then dividing with sum of the precision and recall.
